Schr√∂dinger Equation Simulation
This visualization models the foundational equation of quantum mechanics for a single, non-relativistic particle. It uses the Finite-Difference Time-Domain (FDTD) method, a standard numerical technique, to iteratively solve the 2D time-dependent Schr√∂dinger equation. The environment is defined by a fractal potential energy field, creating barriers and channels that influence the wavefunction's evolution. The simulation demonstrates core quantum phenomena like superposition, quantum tunneling, and interference. The brightness of a pixel corresponds to the probability density of finding the particle, while the hue represents the phase of the complex wavefunction.
